ENERGIZING EDUCATION PROGRAMME (EEP)

A Federal Government of Nigeria initiative providing sustainable, clean power to 37 federal universities and 7 university teaching hospitals nationwide.

About EEP

The Energizing Education Programme (EEP) is a Federal Government of Nigeria initiative, implemented by the Rural Electrification Agency (REA), aimed at providing reliable and sustainable electricity to Federal Universities and its Affiliated Teaching Hospitals through solar hybrid power plants and rehabilitated distribution infrastructure.
The programme also includes the installation/renovation of streetlights to enhance security, establishment of a Renewable Energy Workshop & Training Centres (WTCs) for students to build local capacity, and the training of 20 female STEM students in the design and construction of renewable energy technology towards promoting gender inclusion and diversity in the energy sector.
Implemented in phases, Phase I (FGN-funded) covered 7 Federal Universities, all completed and commissioned
Phase II (World Bank-funded) includes 7 Universities and 2 Teaching Hospitals, with commissioning already underway across all institutions.
Phase III (AfDB-funded) covers 8 Federal Universities and 1 Teaching Hospital, with commissioning targeted for Q4 2025.
Phase IV is currently at its preliminary stage, with collaboration agreements signed between the Rural Electrification Agency (REA) and the 8 Beneficiary Federal institutions to formalize participation and align on implementation pathways.
