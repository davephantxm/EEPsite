Ceremonial Site Handovers Signal the Start of Implementation for EEP Phase III Projects

The Energizing Education Programme (EEP) Phase III entered a critical phase of implementation in February 2024 with the successful ceremonial handovers of project sites across its eight (8) beneficiary institutions and one (1) affiliated teaching hospital. These handovers, rich in symbolism and procedural rigour, marked the formal commencement of works under the African Development Bank-funded phase of the Nigeria Electrification Project (NEP).

Each ceremony followed a structured protocol. The Vice Chancellor of each institution formally handed the site to the Rural Electrification Agency’s (REA) - NEP representative. In turn, the REA-NEP handed over the site to the Project Owner’s Engineer (POE) representative, who then formally transferred responsibility to the EPC contractor on record. The ceremonies concluded with a tour of the proposed solar hybrid power plant sites, offering stakeholders a physical understanding of the scope and vision of the project.

The handover events were conducted as follows:

February 12, 2024: Simultaneous handovers at the Federal University Uyo and the Federal University Lokoja.

February 14, 2024: Ceremony at the Federal University of Technology, Owerri (FUTO).

February 15, 2024: Handover at the Federal University, Dutsin-Ma, Katsina State.

February 20, 2024: Handover at the Federal University Lafia.

February 23, 2024: Simultaneous handovers at the Federal University of Technology, Akure (FUTA) and Modibbo Adama University of Technology, Yola, Adamawa State.

March 16, 2024: Joint handover at the University of Port Harcourt and the University of Port Harcourt Teaching Hospital.

The EEP Phase III is set to deliver solar hybrid power systems, street lighting, rehabilitated distribution infrastructure, and world-class renewable energy training centres to each institution, enhancing learning environments, supporting healthcare services, and aligning with Nigeria’s Energy Transition Plan.

These handovers represent concrete steps toward a cleaner, more resilient academic and medical infrastructure across the nation.
